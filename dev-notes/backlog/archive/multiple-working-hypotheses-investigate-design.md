## The finding (well-sourced, peer-reviewed)
Chamberlin's "method of multiple working hypotheses" (1897, Science) + Platt's Strong Inference
(1964): carry **several rival designs in parallel** during investigate-design, none personally owned
→ structurally neutralizes the "parental affection" / confirmation-bias attachment that biases a
single-hypothesis investigator toward pressing facts to fit, AND forces completeness (co-equal
hypotheses probe lines a single hypothesis overlooks). Verbatim: "The investigator thus becomes the
parent of a family of hypotheses; and, by his parental relation to all, he is forbidden to fasten
his affections unduly upon any one."

## Why anneal lacks it (verified against the live corpus by the run)
`investigate-design.md` drives to a SINGLE committed recommendation; the operator is never posed
competing alternatives; the only multi-alternative construct (the optional, post-hoc one-line
"considered" field) is not a mandate to carry rivals in parallel; the falsification pass probes an
already-LOCKED single decision, not rival designs during formation. **Partial mitigation anneal
already has:** separate-context independent verification attacks the SAME attachment bias from a
different angle (the falsifier has no parental affection) — but it fires AFTER commit, not during
design formation.

## The design tension to resolve FIRST (the real question)
anneal **deliberately** avoids posing the operator a menu (committed-recommendation design; avoids
menu-paralysis). Chamberlin wants rivals carried co-equally. **Can rivals be carried INTERNALLY
during investigate-design — capturing the de-biasing + completeness — while still surfacing ONE
committed recommendation to the operator?** Or do they genuinely conflict? (Leaning: compatible — the
discipline is about the *investigator's process*, not the *operator's interface* — but that is
exactly what must be sharpened before any edit.)

## Caution (don't over-import)
Adopt the multiple-hypothesis DISCIPLINE, NOT Strong Inference's discredited promises (a single
crucial experiment settles it; complete hypothesis enumeration) — refuted by O'Donohue & Buchanan +
Quine-Duhem. Quine-Duhem actually reinforces anneal's recheck/loopback.
